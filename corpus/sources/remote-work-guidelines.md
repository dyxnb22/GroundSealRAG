# Remote Work Guidelines

These guidelines define expectations for employees who work remotely full-time or on a hybrid schedule. They cover availability, communication, home office setup, security, and performance standards. All remote employees must comply regardless of country unless a local addendum specifies otherwise.

## Eligibility and Work Arrangements

Remote work is available for roles that do not require daily on-site presence. Managers document approved arrangement type: fully remote, hybrid with specified office days, or temporary remote with end date. Changes require HR and manager approval to ensure tax and legal compliance.

Contractors with limited access receive general-visibility guidance documents such as this one; system access remains governed by separate agreements.

## Core Hours and Availability

Predictable overlap hours enable collaboration across time zones. Employees must be reachable during declared core hours unless on approved leave.

**Remote work core hours:** GroundSeal core hours are 10:00 to 16:00 in the employee's local time zone, Monday through Friday. Employees must be online and responsive on chat during core hours, attend scheduled meetings, and complete assigned work outside core hours flexibly.

Employees outside overlapping zones with their team must shift core hours by mutual agreement documented with the manager, maintaining at least four hours overlap with primary collaborators.

Respond to urgent messages within thirty minutes during core hours. Outside core hours, on-call rotations follow the incident runbook for eligible roles.

## Communication Expectations

Default to asynchronous written updates in project channels. Use video for complex discussions, retrospectives, and relationship building. Status updates should be visible without requiring live meetings for every decision.

Camera use in meetings is encouraged but not mandatory except for customer-facing roles when brand standards apply. Recordings require participant consent per regional privacy rules.

## Home Office Equipment

GroundSeal supports ergonomic and productive home workspaces within defined limits.

**Home office equipment policy:**

- Full-time remote employees receive a standard kit: laptop, one external monitor up to twenty-seven inches, keyboard, mouse, and headset upon hire through IT onboarding.
- Additional monitor or sit-stand desk reimbursement up to four hundred dollars annually with manager approval and receipt submission.
- Internet connectivity is supported via payroll stipend where eligible; employees must maintain broadband sufficient for video calls and VPN usage.
- IT-approved accessories only for devices connecting to corporate networks; personal routers must not bridge unmanaged devices into VPN sessions.
- Equipment remains company property; return upon termination per offboarding checklist.

Employees secure equipment against theft and unauthorized household access. Do not leave company laptops unlocked in shared spaces.

## Workspace Safety and Ergonomics

Maintain a dedicated workspace free from excessive noise during meetings. Follow basic ergonomic guidance: chair height, monitor at eye level, regular breaks. Workers' compensation may apply to work-related injuries at the home office; report incidents to HR immediately.

## Information Security at Home

Remote work does not relax security obligations. Use company-managed devices for work data. Prohibit household members from using work laptops. Lock screens when stepping away. Store physical documents with confidential information in secured locations.

Public Wi-Fi requires VPN or zero-trust client activation before accessing internal systems. Printing confidential documents at home should be avoided; shred if unavoidable.

## Performance and Visibility

Remote employees are evaluated on outcomes, not idle time online. Managers set clear deliverables and provide regular feedback. Visibility comes from documented progress, not constant presence indicators.

## Travel and Temporary Relocation

Working from a different region for more than fourteen consecutive days may trigger tax and legal review. Request approval before travel that changes work location country or state.

## Office Use for Hybrid Employees

Hybrid employees reserve desk space through the office booking system on scheduled in-office days. Team events may require occasional travel; travel expenses follow the expense reimbursement policy.

## Wellbeing and Boundaries

Employees should define shutdown rituals to prevent burnout. Paid time off must be taken and approved through the HR system. Managers monitor workload sustainability during one-on-ones.

## Support Resources

IT helpdesk supports remote provisioning and troubleshooting. HR answers policy questions through `#hr-helpdesk`. Employee assistance program contacts appear in the benefits portal.

## Contractor and Temporary Arrangements

Contractors approved for remote engagement must acknowledge these guidelines during onboarding even when their system access is limited. Contractors follow the same remote work core hours overlap rules with their GroundSeal sponsor unless the statement of work specifies alternative windows.

## Meeting Norms for Distributed Teams

Schedule meetings during overlapping core hours when participants span multiple zones. Agenda and pre-read materials should arrive at least four hours before decision meetings. Record action items in shared documents accessible to absent team members rather than relying on verbal summaries alone.

## Guidelines Maintenance

HR owns these guidelines with input from IT and legal. Updates communicate through `#announcements` with summary of changes affecting core hours or equipment entitlements. Annual refresh confirms home office equipment stipend amounts and core hours still match operating needs across regions. Managers reinforce expectations during team meetings after each revision.
