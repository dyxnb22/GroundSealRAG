# Expense Reimbursement Policy

This policy defines eligible expenses, submission requirements, approval workflows, and reimbursement limits for GroundSeal employees. It applies to business travel, client entertainment, home office purchases, and other work-related spending paid personally and submitted for reimbursement.

## Policy Objectives

GroundSeal reimburses reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred in good faith. The company does not reimburse personal expenses, duplicated claims, or costs lacking adequate documentation. Managers and finance share responsibility for compliance.

## General Eligibility Rules

Expenses must be:

- Incurred for legitimate business purposes directly related to GroundSeal operations.
- Supported by itemized receipts showing vendor, date, amount, and payment proof unless a documented exception applies.
- Submitted through the expense management system; email receipts alone are insufficient.
- Approved before payment processing except emergency travel provisions below.

Alcohol may be reimbursed only for client entertainment with prior manager approval and within per-person caps defined in the travel addendum.

## Receipt Requirements and Submission Deadlines

Accurate documentation protects employees and the company during audits. All claims require receipts meeting minimum readability standards.

**Expense reimbursement receipt deadline:** Employees must submit expenses with valid receipts within thirty calendar days of the transaction date. Claims submitted after the thirty-day receipt deadline require director approval and may be denied if budgeting periods have closed. Exceptions for extended travel must be requested before the deadline expires.

Receipts must show:

- Merchant name and location.
- Transaction date matching the claim.
- Itemized charges, not only summary totals, for meals and lodging.
- Currency and amount paid; foreign expenses include conversion documentation from the system.

Lost receipt affidavits are permitted once per quarter for amounts under twenty-five dollars with manager attestation. Larger lost-receipt claims require finance review and are not guaranteed approval.

## Reimbursement Limits

Reimbursement limits cap spending by category to align with budget and fairness across roles.

**Standard reimbursement limits (USD equivalent):**

- Domestic meals while traveling: seventy-five dollars per day unless city tier exceptions apply.
- International meals: per diem tables in the travel addendum by country.
- Client entertainment: one hundred fifty dollars per person per event with business justification.
- Mileage: IRS standard rate when using personal vehicle for approved business travel.
- Home internet stipend: handled through payroll, not expense reports, for eligible remote employees.
- Individual equipment purchases: require pre-approval; ad hoc hardware over two hundred fifty dollars is not reimbursable without IT ticket linkage.

Executive team members follow the same category limits unless board-approved policies state otherwise.

## Approval Workflow

Employees submit claims; managers approve within five business days. Finance performs secondary review for flags such as policy exceptions, high amounts, or missing documentation. Reimbursement deposits typically occur within ten business days after finance approval.

Rejected claims return to submitter with reason codes. Resubmission must address all cited issues.

## Travel-Specific Guidance

Air travel should be economy class for flights under six hours unless medical or accessibility needs require upgrade. Book through approved travel tools when available to capture corporate discounts and duty-of-care data.

Hotels should be mid-tier business properties near work sites. Laundry is reimbursable only for trips exceeding five consecutive nights.

## Non-Reimbursable Items

The following are generally not reimbursable:

- Commuting between home and regular worksite except approved temporary assignments.
- Personal entertainment, gym memberships, or childcare except where legally mandated.
- Traffic fines, parking tickets, and legal penalties.
- Gifts to employees without HR pre-approval.
- Software subscriptions available through corporate licenses.

## Corporate Card Alternative

Employees with corporate cards must prefer card payment over personal reimbursement for eligible spend. Corporate card policies mirror receipt and coding requirements without the personal outlay step.

## Tax and Payroll Implications

Some reimbursements may be taxable depending on jurisdiction and categorization. Payroll notifies employees when stipends are treated as wages. International employees consult local HR for variant rules.

## Audits and Fraud Prevention

Finance samples claims monthly for documentation quality and policy adherence. Intentional misrepresentation may result in repayment demand and disciplinary action up to termination.

## Questions and Exceptions

Policy questions route to `#hr-helpdesk`. Exception requests require written business case, manager approval, and HR operations sign-off before incurring cost when possible. Finance publishes a monthly FAQ summarizing common denial reasons, including late submissions past the expense reimbursement receipt deadline, to reduce resubmission cycles.

## Record Retention

Approved expense records and receipts are retained for seven years for audit purposes. Employees should not delete confirmation emails until finance marks the claim paid and archived.

## Currency and International Claims

Employees traveling internationally should enter expenses in the currency paid; the system converts using the corporate rate table on submission date. VAT receipts must be retained where tax reclamation applies. Personal card foreign transaction fees are not reimbursable unless pre-approved for regions without corporate card acceptance.

## Policy Maintenance

HR and finance co-own this policy. Updates effective at the start of each fiscal quarter unless urgent regulatory changes require immediate adoption. Employees receive email summary when reimbursement limits or the expense reimbursement receipt deadline rules change.
