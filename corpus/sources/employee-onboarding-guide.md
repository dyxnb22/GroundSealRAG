# Employee Onboarding Guide

This guide helps HR coordinators and hiring managers onboard new GroundSeal employees during their first weeks. It covers administrative setup, IT provisioning, training requirements, and cultural integration. Contractors follow a shortened checklist unless otherwise noted.

## Before Day One

Complete pre-boarding tasks at least three business days before start date.

- Send welcome email with start time, location or remote instructions, and manager contact.
- Create employee record in the HR information system with correct department, manager, and work location.
- Trigger IT provisioning ticket with role, laptop preference, and required application access.
- Assign onboarding buddy unrelated to direct reporting line.
- Schedule day-one meetings: HR orientation, manager one-on-one, team introduction.
- Prepare payroll and benefits enrollment links where applicable.

Verify background check and signed offer letter status before granting system access.

## First Week Checklist

The first week checklist ensures new hires become productive safely without overwhelming them. HR tracks completion in the onboarding tracker.

**Day 1 — first week checklist items:**

- Complete HR orientation covering policies, code of conduct, and harassment prevention training.
- Sign required policy acknowledgments including security, remote work, and expense policies.
- Collect laptop and peripherals from IT or confirm home shipment delivery.
- Log into email, calendar, chat, and HR portal; enable multi-factor authentication.
- Meet manager for role expectations, ninety-day goals, and team rituals.
- Meet onboarding buddy for informal Q&A and lunch or virtual coffee.
- Join standard channels: `#announcements`, department channel, and `#new-hires`.

**Days 2–3:**

- Complete security awareness training module.
- Set up development environment if in engineering; follow coding standards repository readme.
- Review org chart and key cross-functional partners.
- Attend team standup and listen before taking assignment ownership.

**Days 4–5:**

- Complete benefits enrollment if within eligibility window.
- Shadow customer-facing or internal workflows relevant to role.
- Document open questions for end-of-week check-in with manager.
- HR confirms all day-one administrative tasks closed in tracker.

Managers add role-specific items but must not skip mandatory compliance training.

## IT Setup

IT setup standardizes device and access provisioning. Tickets use the onboarding template with employee ID and start date.

**Standard IT setup deliverables:**

- Company laptop with full-disk encryption and endpoint management agent installed.
- External monitor, keyboard, and headset for full-time remote employees upon request.
- Accounts: email, SSO identity, chat, source control, ticket system, and VPN or zero-trust client if required.
- Role-based access requests approved by manager for production systems; default deny until training complete.
- Password manager organization vault invitation.

Engineering hires receive additional access to CI systems and staging environments after security training completion. Support hires receive CRM and telephony credentials after privacy training.

Report lost or defective equipment within twenty-four hours through the IT helpdesk portal.

## HR and Payroll Orientation

HR explains pay schedule, time tracking expectations, holidays, and leave policies. New hires confirm personal details, emergency contacts, and tax withholding forms. Questions about compensation belong to HR business partners, not managers, for consistency.

International hires follow the regional addendum for local statutory requirements and public holiday calendars.

## Manager Responsibilities

Managers prepare a written thirty-sixty-ninety day plan with learning objectives and measurable outcomes. Introduce the new hire to stakeholders within the first two weeks. Schedule weekly one-on-ones during the first month.

Managers must not assign production on-call shifts during week one unless pre-agreed for senior hires with prior system familiarity.

## Training and Compliance Deadlines

Mandatory training modules due within the first fourteen days include security awareness, code of conduct, and data handling overview for roles touching customer data. Overdue training triggers automatic access restrictions per security policy.

## Payroll and Benefits Deep Dive

During HR orientation, new hires learn how to enroll in health benefits within thirty days of eligibility and how to designate beneficiaries for retirement plans where offered. Payroll explains direct deposit setup, pay stub access, and how overtime or regional holiday pay applies to their employment classification.

## First Month Milestones

By end of month one, employees should understand team goals, have completed mandatory training, and deliver a small contribution such as documentation fix, bug triage, or project scoping document. HR conducts thirty-day pulse survey and addresses friction points. Managers document progress against the ninety-day plan in the talent system for visibility across leadership.

## Offboarding Reference

This guide focuses on onboarding. Offboarding procedures live in the separate termination checklist. Managers must notify HR immediately upon resignation or role change affecting access scope.

## Accessibility and Accommodations

New hires requiring workplace accommodations should contact HR before day one so IT and facilities can provision appropriate equipment or schedule adjustments. Accommodation requests are confidential and processed separately from performance discussions.

## Guide Maintenance

HR operations owns this guide. Process changes publish with effective date and summary email to hiring managers. Feedback from new hires is collected at thirty days and incorporated quarterly. The first week checklist is version-controlled alongside this document so coordinators always distribute current steps.
